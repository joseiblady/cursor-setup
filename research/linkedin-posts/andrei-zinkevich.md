- hit your awareness criteria and engagement threshold

2/ TARGET ACCOUNT WEBSITE VISITS.

- Which reps drove the most engagement

During the program, we collect anecdotes from the sales reps to 
prove the program's impact.

Here are 8 pillars of the full-funnel ABM strategy we use in 
2026 for B2B SaaS companies with long sales cycles:

1/ CLUSTER TARGETING.

Cluster targeting allows:
- Nurture the entire cluster of ICP accounts despite of the tier 
  (tier 1, tier 2, tier 3) and status (new logo, expansion, 
  pipeline account)
- Create a relevant message and content
- Focus on accounts with a high-probability of becoming a sales 
  opportunity

2/ CROSS-FUNCTIONAL TEAM.

We replace the disconnected departments with integrated revenue 
teams that share the same go-to-market strategy, playbooks, and 
revenue goals.

Here is the lean, cross-functional team structure:
- ABM lead
- Account-based sales rep / SDR
- Content marketer
- SME
- Supporting Roles: AE + RevOps + product.

3/ 6A FRAMEWORK TO DESIGN AN ABM PROGRAM.

We use the 6A framework to orchestrate personalized, continuous 
nurturing for strategic accounts across the entire buyer journey. 
It consists of:
- Account qualification
- Account segmentation and prioritization
- Account research
- Account awareness
- Account development (nurturing)
- Account activation

4/ PLAYBOOKS OPTIMIZED FOR LONG SALES CYCLES.

We mix Always On, time-based and signal-based playbook and run 
them in parallel:

a) Time-based touchpoints.
These are planned activities mapped out over a quarter and synced 
with Always On programs to stay top-of-mind and deliver value 
regardless of where the account is in their buyer journey.

b) Signal-based touchpoints.
These are reactive plays triggered by specific buying signals 
that allow your team to spark highly relevant conversations at 
exactly the right moment.

5/ CREATE CENTER OF EXCELLENCE.

Center of Excellence (COE) is a dedicated team that owns the 
ABM strategy, execution quality, and scaling of the ABM program. 
It operates on 60/20 model:
- 80% of the time: operationalization, documentation, scaling, 
  and training.
- 20% of the time: innovation and experimentation.

6/ AI-ENABLED PROGRAM.

An AI-enabled (not AI-created and executed) ABM program relies 
on the 80/20 rule: AI handles 80% of the legwork (like data 
collection and signal capturing), while humans add the crucial 
20% of strategic insight and relationship building.

7/ HOLISTIC MEASUREMENT ACROSS THE FULL FUNNEL.

Here are 6 ABM reports that show the program's impact we share 
with leadership:
- Total revenue by programs and tiers.
- Pipeline progress.
- Program performance.
- Sales engagement metrics.
- Account engagement.
- Account development progress.

8/ COORDINATION RHYTHM: QUARTERLY-WEEKLY-DAILY SYNC MEETINGS.

To avoid the common trap of being "aligned in theory but siloed 
in practice," we establish a strict operational rhythm with 
quarterly planning sessions & retrospective, weekly planning & 
sync meetings, and daily standups.

This simple ABM playbook helped us to book meetings with private 
banking leaders ($2 mln average deal size):

I'll skip the essential parts of ABM strategy, account selection 
and buying committee mapping processes here, and dive straight 
into the playbook.

1/ ACCOUNT RESEARCH TO DEVELOP OUR POINT OF VIEW.

- What strategic projects are they investing now relevant to our 
  product?
- What's been changing/trending last year relevant to our 
  product? Why?
- What challenges do they face?
- What metric do we want to influence on the account level, 
  and how?

The entire goal of this research is to understand what narrative 
will be relevant for this specific account. It defines the 
content, the events, and the messaging.

2/ CONNECTIONS AND ENGAGEMENT WITH THE ENTIRE BUYING COMMITTEE.

People buy from people they know, like and trust.

If nobody knows us, doesn't matter how personalized our emails, 
ads and messages are. Nobody is going to reply.

3/ PUBLISHING ACCOUNT LOVE LETTER.

Account love letters are LinkedIn posts that feature target 
accounts. We use insights from the account research and our POV 
to highlight a company's strategic moves, recent hires, 
acquisitions, investments, or initiatives.

The goal is simple: create awareness and start a conversation, 
not to do a sales pitch.

4/ DISTRIBUTING LOVE LETTER TO ALL BUYING COMMITTEE MEMBERS.

Basic:
- Sales reps send a link to the post in DM + email.
- Marketing boosts love letter with thought leadership ads.
- Do follow ups with the next relevant CTA (e.g. inviting to 
  the event)

Advanced:
Use Influ2 to deliver personalized message with relevant CTA 
for every buyer.

5/ DEVELOP PERSONALIZED WEEKLY ENGAGEMENT PLAN FOR EVERY BUYING 
COMMITTEE MEMBER.

Love letters are just one touchpoint. But we need to consistently 
stay top of the minds.

We map first:
- Buying committee role
- KPI or top metric the buyer owns
- Known (or assumed) challenges

Then, develop a relevant engagement plan to stay in touch across 
a long sales cycle.

If you want to replicate it, here are some rules:

1. Orchestrate your program on contact level.
Think where different buying committee members are in their 
journey right now? What is your relationship with them? What 
would be the most valuable next step?

2. Fit your solution into their strategic project.
You're competing for the attention today. If your buyer doesn't 
see how any of your assets fit their strategic initiatives, they 
will ignore it. Period.

3. Optimize for long-term sales cycle.
If your sales cycle length is 18 months, you'll never generate 
a sales opportunity today from 5-7 emails and air cover for 30 
days. Optimize for long term nurturing.

## Key Patterns Observed

- **Radical methodology transparency as the primary sales tool:** 
  All three posts share the exact frameworks FullFunnel uses with 
  paying clients — the 8 ABM pillars, the account love letter 
  playbook, the Future Pipeline Accounts metric. This is a 
  deliberate strategy: by publishing the full methodology, he 
  pre-qualifies prospects who read it and think "this is exactly 
  what we need." The people who hire him already understand his 
  approach before the first call. For 100Hires, the equivalent 
  would be publishing the exact hiring workflow their platform 
  automates — turning product education into demand generation.

- **Numbered frameworks as the dominant format:** Every post 
  uses the same structure — a numbered list of pillars, steps, 
  or data points, each with a clear label in caps. This is 
  not accidental: it signals that the content is organized, 
  complete, and actionable. Readers know exactly what they're 
  getting before they read past the first line. The format also 
  makes posts highly saveable — readers bookmark frameworks they 
  want to reference later, which drives the save signals 
  LinkedIn's algorithm rewards.

- **Pipeline language, not marketing language:** Zinkevich never 
  describes his work as "content" or "brand building." He talks 
  about Future Pipeline Accounts, buying committee engagement, 
  revenue attribution, and sales cycle optimization. This is a 
  conscious positioning choice: it makes his content legible to 
  CEOs and Sales Directors — the people who control budget — not 
  just marketers. Any B2B SaaS company trying to get content 
  strategy taken seriously at the executive level should study 
  how he frames the value of organic LinkedIn presence in pure 
  revenue terms.
