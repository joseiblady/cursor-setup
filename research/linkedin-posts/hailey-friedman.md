## Key Patterns Observed

- **Early mover authority on a specific channel shift:** All 
  three posts are about GEO — a topic she committed to before 
  most practitioners had even heard the term. This is the most 
  durable LinkedIn content strategy: identify a genuine shift 
  early, document your learning in public, and by the time the 
  mainstream catches up you're the reference point. For 100Hires, 
  the equivalent would be owning the conversation around 
  AI-powered hiring before their competitors do.

- **Third-party validation as the primary credibility lever:** 
  The NYT mention appears across multiple posts — not as 
  bragging, but as proof that her thesis was validated externally. 
  She uses it once to tell the story, then references it again 
  as context. This is deliberate: in B2B content, a single 
  credible external validation is worth more than ten 
  self-published claims.

- **Data from client work as content fuel:** Posts 1 and 3 
  are built entirely on proprietary data — 35,000 backlinks 
  analyzed, 21,000 brand mentions studied. She turns her 
  agency's operational work into public insights, which means 
  her content costs essentially nothing to produce beyond the 
  work she's already doing. This is the most sustainable B2B 
  content model: let the client work generate the research, 
  then publish the findings.
