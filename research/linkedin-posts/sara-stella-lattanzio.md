## Key Patterns Observed

- **Criticism as credibility:** Lattanzio's most engaging content 
  isn't advice — it's critique. She calls out lazy practices by name 
  (comment-bait posts, guru advice, vanity metrics) and her audience 
  responds because it feels like someone finally saying what they're 
  thinking. For B2B SaaS, this pattern is instructive: taking a clear 
  position on an industry frustration generates more trust than 
  sharing another framework.

- **Personal narrative as GTM signal:** Post 2 isn't a career update 
  — it's a positioning statement. By describing what she actually 
  works on (GTM alignment, ABM, content strategy) through the lens 
  of a life update, she signals expertise without pitching. This is 
  one of the most effective organic lead generation patterns on 
  LinkedIn and almost impossible to fake.

- **Polls as conversation starters, not vanity metrics:** Her poll 
  isn't there for engagement — it's there to surface the audience's 
  frustration and validate her thesis. The comments become the real 
  content. This is a sophisticated use of LinkedIn's native formats 
  that most B2B brands completely miss.
