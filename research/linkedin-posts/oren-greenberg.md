## Key Patterns Observed

- **Original data as the ultimate content moat:** Post 3 is a 
  masterclass in data-driven content — he tracked 101 companies 
  himself and turned the findings into a post that no one else 
  could have written. This is the highest-trust content format on 
  LinkedIn because it's impossible to fake. For a company like 
  100Hires operating in the recruitment space, this pattern is 
  directly replicable: original data about hiring trends would 
  resonate immediately with their exact ICP.

- **Extreme brevity as a signal of authority:** Greenberg's posts 
  are among the shortest on this list. He never over-explains. 
  This creates a specific reader experience — you feel like you're 
  getting insider shorthand, not a tutorial. That tone attracts 
  senior buyers and repels tire-kickers, which is precisely the 
  audience a B2B SaaS company wants to build.

- **Giving away high-value assets to build authority:** Post 2 
  explicitly describes his own content strategy — produce something 
  genuinely useful (a Miro template, a GitHub repo) and distribute 
  it for free. He doesn't just teach this; he demonstrates it in 
  the same post by linking to his Miroverse template. This 
  self-referential quality — showing rather than telling — is one 
  of the most credible content moves on the platform.
