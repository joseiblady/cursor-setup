I've been feeling a weight on my shoulders for the past few weeks.

This is something new for me.

For the past 8 years, I've been working by myself, for myself, and 
I didn't have any employees.

This meant everything that was happening in my business was my 
responsibility.

Since I launched The Sales Creator Collective, and especially since

I now represent around 65 creators, and the expectations that come 
from it is sometimes too much for me.

The expectation to always be on.

The expectation to always deliver new deals.

The expectation to be the best negotiator in the world.

All of this is wearing me down for a simple reason: I don't want to 
disappoint people who put their trust in me.

But I can't please everyone, and some days are a constant flow of 
complaints and putting out fires while fighting my natural tendency 
to be brutally direct (and hurt feelings in the process).

This creates a lot of background anxiety, and I spend way too much 
time worrying about problems before they even appear.

But in the same time, I'm learning so much and I'm getting to work 
closely with so many incredible people.

I'm also having a real, positive impact on people.

Some creators left their full-time job to go all-in on their 
businesses because of the money we made together.

Some are planning big moves and I'm the one they go to for advice 
and ideas.

This is everything I've always dreamed of.

But the cost could get too high if I let my negative inner voice win.

And some days it does.

If you've been in this situation, I'd love to hear your advice.

How to close 12-months contracts as a sales creator:

Step 1: Accept most brand deals

When you start working with brands as a sales creator, you don't 
really have the luxury to be picky.

You don't have a track record, influencer managers don't know what 
to expect from you, and you are mostly experimenting to see what 
works.

That's why you need to accept most brand deals that come your way.

You need data to understand:
- what format works best
- what your audience is interested in
- what type of results you can deliver for the brands

Step 2: Overdeliver through affiliate links

Most brands that work with sales creators have affiliate programs 
that reward you with cash or a percentage of a deal they closed with 
a customer that signed up through your link.

In 80% of cases, they won't even require you to use this affiliate 
link, but you can ask them to sign you up on their affiliate program.

When you have your link, systematically include it in your sponsored 
posts for the brand, and in any piece of content where it can be 
relevant.

This will do two things:
1. You will make more money if someone converts thanks to your link
2. Your will create undebatable performance data you can use to 
   negotiate further

Step 3: Document your results

Most brands have no clue how to structure an influence campaign, and 
they don't even ask you to share data after you've posted for them.

Systematically document the following elements:
- Impressions
- Engagement
- Saves & sends on LinkedIn
- Signups, DMs, conversions, etc.

If you do this, you'll stand out and do more for the brands than 99% 
of creators they work with.

Influencer managers absolutely LOVE working with creators who are 
well-organized.

Step 4: Focus on lead gen content

I've been having conversations with many CMOs and Brand Marketing 
Managers and they all tell me the same thing:

There's too much brand awareness sponsored content (the one that 
make people think about the brand) and not enough lead generation 
content (the one that generates leads).

The best way to turn your audience into qualified traffic and 
conversions for your sponsors is to create a long-form piece of 
content and distribute it to your audience.

For example, you could do the following:
- 5-min video explaining how to create a prospecting sequence that 
  gets replies
- This video is then transcribed to create a long-form blog post
- The video is also cut into smaller vertical videos
- Turn the long-form blog post into a lead magnet (ideal for viral 
  drop LinkedIn posts)
- Use LinkedIn and newsletter to promote these various elements
- All these assets (videos, blog posts, lead magnets, etc.) can be 
  used for ads
- Put your affiliate link everywhere in these assets

This requires more work than creating a LinkedIn post, but you can 
get paid a lot more for doing it.

And if you need help with these 4 steps, drop me a DM.

Yesterday I wrote a newsletter about a concept:

Intellectual Sovereignty.

I define Intellectual Sovereignty as the supreme power or authority 
an individual has over their thoughts and ideas.

I'm about to get philosophical, but stay with me.

When most of your thoughts come from an Artificial Intelligence, or 
from scrolling, you're not in control of your thoughts anymore.

You're reactive.

You get constantly interrupted by notifications, and you live to 
chase your next cheap dopamine hit.

This is what I'm observing with people who divide their time between 
consuming social media during their free time, and mostly using AI 
when they need to work.

They can't think for themselves anymore. And, in my opinion, it's a 
good first step to getting enslaved by technology.

I have no doubts about what this means for the future of humanity.

Most people are already being told what to think, who to vote for, 
and what to buy.

AI is only accelerating this trend, and the vast majority of people 
have no real way out.

But if you want to fight this trend, you need to reclaim your 
Intellectual Sovereignty.

This doesn't mean you should live without social medias and AI, but 
here are concrete things I do to protect my Intellectual Sovereignty:

- I don't use AI to write or brainstorm
- I hang out with real people, in real life
- I remove all notifications from social media
- I travel and get exposed to other cultures and people
- I get my information from news outlet with completely opposed 
  political views. I've realized they are using facts to engineer 
  a truth that serves their interests.

I love AI and tech, this is what allows me to work from where I 
want, when I want.

But I'm choosing to stay sovereign over my thoughts because I hate 
being told what to do or what to think.

Thoughts?
