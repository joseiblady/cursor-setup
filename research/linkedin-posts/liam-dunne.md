NEW: Our research supported by Ahrefs - Reddit's impact on 
ChatGPT is mostly invisible and heavily influences retrieval.

A few months ago Discovered Labs published our findings from 
research to understand Reddit's impact on ChatGPT, however we 
took a different approach to the methodology.

Most studies on Reddit look at the visible citations in ChatGPT, 
whereas we looked under the hood (by decrypting traffic) to 
understand its hidden influence, here's what we discovered:

Reddit powers ~27% of ChatGPT's web searches but shows up in 
less than 1% of analysed citations.

ChatGPT allocates 16 of its 60 search slots to Reddit via 
Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF) which our research found to be 
more than Bing.

As you can see from the attached screenshot, Ahrefs' research 
doesn't seem to be far off from that either :)

It feels good to see big names validate our research and prove 
our R&D efforts are working.

Why this matters:

If ChatGPT relies on Reddit for answer grounding it's in your 
best interest to have a high share of voice in the subreddits 
and threads it's likely pulling from.

Higher share of voice = you get to shape the narrative, not 
competitors.
