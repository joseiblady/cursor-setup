Not all LinkedIn posts die the same way.
Some explode and vanish. Others build slowly. A few even come back 
from the dead.

After analysing 1.3M posts, we found 4 distinct lifespan patterns:

🚀 The Rocket (45% of posts)
Strong out of the gate. 40% of reach lands within 4 hours, 70% within 
day one. Burns fast — done by day 5.

How to spot it early:
— Engagement floods in within the first hour
— Comments spike immediately after posting
— Reach plateaus by hour 4

How to nurture it:
— Reply to every comment in the first 4 hours
— Ask follow-up questions to keep threads alive
— Don't wait — the window closes fast

⚙️ The Builder (30% of posts)
Modest start, steady climb. Engagement builds through day 1 and 
sustains to day 8. Save rates are disproportionately high.

How to spot it early:
— Slow initial reactions but steady saves
— Comments trickle in over hours, not minutes
— Reach keeps climbing after hour 4

How to nurture it:
— Re-enter the thread on days 2-3 with a fresh insight
— Reply to comments with depth, not just "thanks"
— Share the post to your newsletter or other channels

🌱 The Grower (20% of posts)
Slow to start, long to run. Only 30% reach on day one, then pulses 
across two weeks driven by saves and interest cluster resurfacing.

How to spot it early:
— Low initial engagement but high save-to-like ratio
— New comments appearing 48+ hours later
— Reach graph shows multiple small spikes

How to nurture it:
— Don't abandon after 48 hours — it's just warming up
— Comment something valuable on day 3-4 to trigger redistribution
— Let it breathe — don't post over it too quickly

🔥 The Afterburner (5% of posts)
Underperforms days 1-2, then ignites. A share, comment, or save 
triggers a spike on day 3-4 and sustained distribution for two weeks.

How to spot it early:
— Disappointing first 48 hours
— Sudden spike in saves or shares on day 2-3
— Comments from outside your usual audience

How to nurture it:
— Double down when it ignites — reply aggressively
— Add a comment with additional context or data
— Share it yourself to accelerate the second wave
